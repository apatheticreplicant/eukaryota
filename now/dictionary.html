bunch’a words worth knowing.

nouns §

sedition /sĭ-dĭsh′ən/ §

  1. Conduct or language inciting rebellion against the authority of a state.
  2. Insurrection; rebellion.
  3. A factious commotion in a state; the stirring up of such a commotion; incitement of discontent against government and disturbance of public tranquillity, as by inflammatory speeches or writings, or acts or language tending to breach of public order: as, to stir up a sedition; a speech or pamphlet, abounding in sedition.

pareidolia /păr″ī-dō′lē-ə/ §

  1. The perception of a recognizable image or meaningful pattern where none exists or is intended, as the perception of a face in the surface features of the moon.
  2. The tendency to interpret a vague stimulus as something known to the observer, such as interpreting marks on Mars as canals, seeing shapes in clouds, or hearing hidden messages in music.

homily /hŏm′ə-lē/ §

  1. A sermon, especially one intended to explain the practical and moral implications of a particular scriptural passage.
  2. A moralizing lecture or admonition that is often tedious or condescending.
  3. A platitudinous or inspirational saying.

ephemera /ĭ-fĕm′ər-ə/ §

  1. A fever which lasts but a day or a very short period.
  2. Anything very short-lived.
  3. Plural of ephemeron

ephemeron /ĭ-fĕm′ə-rŏn″/ §

  1. A short-lived thing.
  2. Printed matter of passing interest.
  3. An insect which lives but for a day or for a very short time; hence, any being whose existence is very brief.

perspicacity /pûr″spĭ-kăs′ĭ-tē/ §

  1. Acuteness of perception, discernment, or understanding.
  2. The state or character of being perspicacious.
  3. Acuteness of discernment or understanding; penetration; sagacity: as, a man of great perspicacity.

senescence /sɪˈnɛsəns/ §

  1. The condition of growing old, or of decaying by time; decadence.
  2. The state of growing old; decay by time.
  3. The state or process of ageing, especially in humans; old age.

adjectives §

itinerant /ī-tĭn′ər-ənt, ĭ-tĭn′-/ §

  1. Traveling from place to place, especially to perform work or a duty. ”an itinerant judge; itinerant labor.”
  2. One who travels from place to place, particularly a preacher; one who is unsettled.
  3. Passing or traveling about a country; going or preaching on a circuit; wandering; not settled. ”an itinerant preacher; an itinerant peddler.”

Similar: wandering

arterial /är-tîr′ē-əl/ §

  1. Of, like, or in an artery or arteries.
  2. Of, relating to, or being the blood in the arteries that has absorbed oxygen in the lungs and is bright red.
  3. Being a main road or channel with many branches. “an arterial highway; an arterial route.”

maudlin /môd′lĭn/ §

  1. Effusively sad or full of self-pity; extremely sentimental: synonym: sentimental.
  2. Tearful; easily moved to tears; exciting to tears; excessively sentimental; weak and silly.
  3. Drunk, or somewhat drunk; fuddled; given to drunkenness.
